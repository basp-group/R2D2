Introduction

We present a novel deep learning approach for high-resolution high-dynamic range synthesis imaging by radio interferometry (RI) in astronomy. R2D2, standing for "Residual-to-Residual DNN series for high-Dynamic range imaging", is a model-based data-driven approach relying on deep neural networks (DNNs) and data-consistency updates. R2D2's reconstruction is formed as a series of residual images, iteratively estimated as outputs of DNNs taking the previous iteration's image estimate and associated data residual as inputs. It thus features a hybrid structure between a plug-and-play algorithm and a learned version of the matching pursuit algorithm of which CLEAN is a well-known example. We propose two variants of the R2D2 model, built upon two distinctive DNN architectures: a standard U-Net, and a novel unrolled architecture dubbed R2D2-Net, obtained by unrolling the R2D2 algorithm itself. The capability of the R2D2 algorithm for joint precision and scalability is showcased in simulation, across a variety of image and data settings, and demonstrated on real observations of the radio galaxy Cygnus A at S band with the Very Large Array.
